# Format Competitor Style References

The `video-editor` skill reads this file to apply our format competitors' edit styles to a 100x Marketer video. Update this file whenever we observe new patterns from the listed creators, or when we add a new reference creator.

When in doubt, the spine of every 100x edit is **demo-first, screen-recording-heavy, on-screen text every 4-8 seconds, no fade-in, no intro card, single CTA card at the end**.

**Primary reference:** @sabrina_ramonov. When in doubt about pacing, hook structure, or on-screen text, mirror her. The two supporting references (@kylewhitrow, @mavgpt) are tactical complements for specific beat types.

## @sabrina_ramonov (PRIMARY reference)

**Profile:** [instagram.com/sabrina_ramonov](https://www.instagram.com/sabrina_ramonov/). Solo creator. 1.5M+ followers, 500M+ views, $0 ad spend, no team. Founder of Blotato (AI cross-platform content engine). Audience is creators, marketers, and entrepreneurs using AI to scale content. Brendan named this as the gold-standard reference for the type of videos 100x Marketer should produce.

**Why she is the spine:** She has solved the problem 100x Marketer is teaching. Solo, AI-native, scalable, audience-first. Same banned-word philosophy as our BRAND_VOICE.md (she actively teaches removing em dashes, semicolons, formal transitions to humanize AI writing). She publishes a Viral Hook Generator GPT trained on 1,000+ proven hooks. Her format is the exact bar.

### Visual style

- **Dominant visual:** quick-cut mix of A-roll talking head, screen recordings of AI tools, and text-heavy graphic cards. Most beats are 1-2 seconds.
- **Cut cadence:** very fast. ~1-1.5 seconds per shot is common. The viewer eye never rests.
- **On-screen text:** large, bold, top-third of the frame. Often the literal hook in giant text overlaying whatever's on screen. White or yellow with strong contrast. Snap-cuts in, no soft animation.
- **Captions:** word-by-word, bottom-third, white with thick black stroke. Always present.
- **Color/look:** neutral, slight warmth on A-roll. No heavy LUTs. Crisp lighting on her face shots.
- **A-roll framing:** medium close-up, eye-level, casual but well-lit. Brand-consistent room/desk setup so viewers recognize her instantly.

### Hook style

- **First 1.5 seconds is sacred.** On-screen text appears before Instagram's caption overlay even renders, so the hook reads even with sound off.
- Hook patterns we observe:
  - "I [outcome] in [time] using [tool]" (specific result + tool name)
  - "Stop [common bad practice]. Do [better thing] instead." (contrarian)
  - "The [number] AI [thing] you need" (numbered list with high-specificity)
  - "This [tool/prompt] saves me [hours/days]" (time-savings receipt)
- She repurposes hooks from non-AI niches into AI content (cross-pollination strategy). Viral hooks from cooking, fitness, finance get rewritten for AI/marketing.

### Pacing

- 20-40 seconds typical. She often stays under 30s, especially on TikTok-first content.
- Visible cut every 1-2 seconds. Many beats are sub-second.
- Holds on prompts/configs are SHORTER than @mavgpt (1.5-2 seconds). She compensates with the prompt being repeated in on-screen text and captions, so viewers can still read it.
- Front-loads value: the "here's exactly what to do" line lands by second 5-7, not at the end.

### CTA pattern

- Verbal CTA at the end + on-screen handle/community link.
- Often invites a save or comment ("save this for later", "comment 'prompt' and I'll DM you").
- Daily-or-multiple-times-daily posting cadence is its own form of CTA: the audience expects more.

### Content formula she repeats

- **Quick tip / hack** in 30 seconds: hook → tool reveal → 3-step demo → CTA.
- **Prompt drop:** hook → "here's the prompt" with prompt on screen → quick demo of output → CTA.
- **Tool stack:** hook → list 3-5 tools with logos and one-liners → CTA.
- **Contrarian take:** hook with a banned-practice claim → why it's wrong → what to do instead → CTA.

### What to apply to 100x edits (treat as defaults unless brief overrides)

- Cut cadence floor: 1-1.5 seconds on screen recordings, 2 seconds on A-roll. Faster than our prior baseline.
- Hook in the first 1.5 seconds, not 3. The 3-second rule is a fallback; aim for 1.5.
- On-screen text headline runs the entire video, top-third. Not just on hook beats.
- Captions ALWAYS, never optional.
- Stay under 30 seconds whenever the brief allows it.
- Hook patterns above are the first place to look when writing the brief.
- Repurpose hooks cross-niche. The brief writer should pull a viral hook from a non-AI niche (fitness, cooking, finance, parenting) and rewrite it for the 100x angle.

## @kylewhitrow (Vibe Marketing | Claude Code) — supporting reference

**Profile:** [instagram.com/kylewhitrow](https://www.instagram.com/kylewhitrow/). Bio: "Claude Code | Vibe Marketing." YouTube reference: "My Claude Code System For Viral Instagram Reels."

**Why we study him:** Closest format-and-niche match in the Claude Code lane. Use his patterns specifically for **Receipt-style briefs and screen-recording-heavy beats** where the terminal is the hero.

### Visual style

- **Dominant visual:** screen recording of Claude Code (dark terminal theme) + an editor (VS Code or similar). Talking head is secondary, often picture-in-picture or A-roll cutaways.
- **Cut cadence:** ~1.5-2.5 seconds per shot on screen recording. Faster than the average Reel.
- **On-screen text:** large bold sans, white with subtle drop shadow, positioned in upper-third or center. Text appears with a snap-cut (no soft fade-in).
- **Color/look:** neutral, slight desaturation. Terminal blacks are deep. No film grain or trendy LUTs.
- **A-roll framing:** medium close-up, casual handheld feel. Not a tripod-locked vlog frame.

### Hook style

- Cold open on a result or claim. First 3 seconds is screen content + on-screen text overlay, not a face.
- Hook patterns we observe: "I built [thing] in [time]", "Here's how I [outcome]", "Stop using [tool], use [Claude Code thing] instead".

### Pacing

- 30-50 second videos most common.
- Visible cut every 2-3 seconds, no exceptions.
- Holds on prompts/configs for 2.5-4 seconds (the pause-and-copy moment).

### CTA pattern

- Verbal in the last 4-5 seconds + on-screen handle/community link.
- Cleanly cut, not faded.

### What to apply to 100x edits

- Default to screen-recording-as-primary-visual.
- Cold open with the result, not a face.
- Use Claude Code's dark theme as the natural visual identity. Do not theme over it; the terminal IS the brand.
- 1.5-3 second cut cadence on screen recordings.

## @mavgpt (Maverick Maltin) — supporting reference

**Profile:** [instagram.com/mavgpt](https://www.instagram.com/mavgpt/). 580K followers. Bio framing: AI & ChatGPT, daily prompts/tips/tools.

**Why we study him:** Adjacent niche (ChatGPT, not Claude Code), but he runs the dominant short-form prompt-teaching format. Use his patterns specifically for **Playbook briefs where a prompt is the hero** and the audience needs a longer hold to read and copy it.

### Visual style

- **Dominant visual:** full-screen prompt or chat output on a dark background. The prompt IS the video.
- **Cut cadence:** slower than kylewhitrow when the prompt is on screen (3-5 second holds). Faster on connector beats.
- **On-screen text:** very large, bold, often the prompt itself rendered as the on-screen text. Bottom-third has captions; top has prompt.
- **Color/look:** dark mode everything. High contrast white-on-black for prompts.
- **A-roll:** minimal. Often no face at all; voiceover only. When face appears, it's a small inset.

### Hook style

- The prompt itself is the hook. First 1-2 seconds is the prompt rendered full-screen, voiceover starts immediately: "Pause. Copy this."
- Hook patterns: "Pause and copy this prompt", "This [model] prompt does [outcome]", "Try this in [tool]".

### Pacing

- 25-45 seconds typical.
- The prompt holds for 3-5 seconds (the entire point of the format).
- After the prompt: 1-2 connector beats showing what it produces, then CTA.

### CTA pattern

- "Follow for daily prompts" verbal + handle on screen.
- Daily cadence is the differentiator. He is teaching the audience he ships every day.

### What to apply to 100x edits

- Use this format for any brief whose teaching beat is a Playbook (a prompt or skill the viewer can copy).
- Render prompts as full-screen text overlays, not screen recordings of typing them. Bigger, more readable.
- Holds of 3+ seconds on prompts are required, not optional.
- Voiceover-first edits are valid and often stronger than face-on-camera for prompt-teaching content.

## Default skeleton (Sabrina-spine, most 100x videos)

Default every video to a Sabrina-spine cut and only deviate when the brief has a specific reason. Target total duration: 25-30 seconds.

- **0-1.5s:** Sabrina-style hook. Big top-third on-screen text headline, A-roll close-up OR a tool screen behind it. The hook reads even with sound off.
- **1.5-7s:** Front-loaded value. The "here's exactly what to do" delivered in 2-3 fast cuts (1-2s each). Mix A-roll and screen.
- **7-15s:** The demo. If it's a Receipt brief, lean kylewhitrow (terminal-heavy, 1.5-2s cuts). If it's a Playbook brief, lean mavgpt (prompt held for 1.5-2s with prompt also rendered as on-screen text so it can be paused).
- **15-22s:** Result + the receipt (timestamp, output, screenshot). 1-2s cuts.
- **22-28s:** A-roll close (Brendan, 3-4s) + CTA card (4-5s).

Captions run the entire video. On-screen text headline updates at major beat changes (every 5-7s) but is always visible.

When the brief calls for a 45-50s video, stretch by adding a second demo beat or a contrarian framing beat between sections, never by slowing the cadence.

## Things we explicitly do NOT borrow

- **Fade-ins, zoom intros, logo splashes.** Cold open every time.
- **Talking-head-only intros.** Show the work, not the face, in the first 3 seconds.
- **Generic "viral hook compilation" cadence.** Every cut should serve the teach, not just keep the eye moving.
- **Dramatic music swells.** Music is a quiet bed under VO, never the lead.
- **Trendy filter LUTs / heavy color grades.** Neutral. The terminal and the artifact are the aesthetic.
