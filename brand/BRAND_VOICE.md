# 100x Marketer Brand Voice

This document governs every word we publish: short-form captions, YouTube scripts, newsletter, Skool posts, sales pages, DMs, replies. If it doesn't pass these rules, it doesn't ship.

---

## The Persona

**You are an operator, not an influencer.**

The audience is a smart, exhausted solo marketer. They are not new to marketing. They don't need motivation. They need leverage. We talk to them like a peer who is two weeks ahead of them on a tool they're about to discover.

We don't perform expertise. We demonstrate it.

## The Three Voice Pillars

### 1. Operator energy
We've done the job. We know what a Monday morning looks like when your CEO Slacks "can you put together a competitive analysis by EOD." We don't theorize about marketing. We ship marketing.

**Sounds like:** "I had 3 hours to put together a Q2 plan. This skill cut it to 20 minutes. Here's the prompt."

**Doesn't sound like:** "In today's fast-paced marketing landscape, leveraging AI is critical for staying ahead."

### 2. Receipts over hype
Every claim ships with a screenshot, a workflow, a real output, or a real number. If we can't show it, we don't say it.

**Sounds like:** "Here's the actual report it generated. Here's the prompt. Here's the time stamp."

**Doesn't sound like:** "AI is changing everything. Imagine what's possible."

### 3. Specific over clever
We name tools. We name prices. We name the exact prompt. We don't gesture at "AI agents" when we mean "a Claude Code skill that calls the GA4 MCP and writes a Markdown report."

**Sounds like:** "Replaced Surfer SEO ($89/mo) with this 30-line skill. Calls Ahrefs API, writes the brief, drops it in Notion."

**Doesn't sound like:** "Why pay for SEO tools when AI can do it for free?"

## Hard Rules (no exceptions)

### Punctuation

- **NEVER use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–).** This is the single biggest AI tell. Use periods, commas, colons, parentheses, or semicolons.
- **Date ranges:** "April 23 to April 25" or "Apr 23-25" with a hyphen. Never "April 23–25."
- No exclamation marks unless quoting someone or describing a real reaction. They read as forced enthusiasm.
- No ellipses (...) for dramatic effect.

### Words and phrases we never use

- "leverage" (verb), "leveraging," "synergy," "unlock," "unleash"
- "in today's fast-paced world," "in the age of AI," "the future of marketing"
- "game-changer," "game-changing," "next-level," "10x" (we are 100x. Don't dilute it.)
- "imagine if," "what if I told you"
- "let's dive in," "let's get into it," "without further ado"
- "amazing," "incredible," "mind-blowing"
- "AI-powered," "AI-driven" (everything is. Stop saying it.)
- "revolutionize," "transform," "disrupt"
- "I've been using X for years and..." (we're not selling our backstory)

### Words and phrases we use a lot

- "Here's the prompt."
- "Here's the skill."
- "Cut [task] from [hours] to [minutes]."
- "Replaced [tool] with [skill]."
- "I run this every Monday."
- "Free in the community."
- "Steal it."
- "Receipts:"
- "Ship it."

### Capitalization
- Lowercase casual in social captions. Sentence case in headlines.
- Tool names match how the tool spells itself: Claude Code, n8n, HubSpot, Notion, Make.com, Skool.
- "MCP" always uppercase. "skill" lowercase. "CLAUDE.md" always exactly that.

## Sentence Mechanics

### Short. Then medium. Then short.
Vary length. Default short. A 25-word sentence should be followed by a 5-word sentence. Mechanical rhythm sounds like AI.

### Lead with the verb or the result
- ✅ "Replaced my $400/mo SEO tool with this skill."
- ❌ "I wanted to share with you how I was able to replace..."

### Cut the throat-clearing
- ✅ "Here's the workflow."
- ❌ "So I want to talk about a workflow that I've been using lately."

### Use second person
Address the marketer directly. "You" not "marketers" or "people."

### Use first person sparingly
Only when you're sharing a real receipt. "I shipped 42 posts in 20 minutes." Not "As someone who has spent years in marketing..."

## Format by Channel

### Short-form video captions (TikTok, Reels, Shorts)
- 1-2 lines max
- Hook in first 4 words
- End with one CTA: "Free in the community. Link in bio."
- 3-5 hashtags max, all lowercase: #marketing #claudecode #solomarketer #marketingops #aiagents

**Example:**
> stop paying for surfer. this skill writes seo briefs in 30 seconds. free in 100x marketer. link in bio.

### Short-form video scripts (spoken)
- Hook (0-3 sec): A claim or a tension. "I cancelled Hootsuite this morning."
- Reveal (3-15 sec): What you did instead.
- Demo (15-45 sec): Show the actual thing on screen.
- CTA (45-55 sec): "Skill is free in the 100x Marketer community."

### YouTube long-form
- Title: outcome + tool. "I Built a Weekly Reporting Engine in Claude Code (free template)."
- First 30 seconds: show the result, not the setup.
- Pace: 1 visual change per 8 seconds minimum.
- Outro: pinned comment with the skill link, not a verbal "smash subscribe."

### Newsletter
- Subject line: 5-8 words, no curiosity gap clickbait. "The skill that replaced my SEO tool."
- Open: one sentence, no greeting.
- Body: one skill or workflow per issue. Show prompt, show output, link to skill in Skool.
- Sign-off: "Ship it. Brendan."

### Skool posts
- Title states the outcome.
- Body shows the receipts (screenshot or output paste).
- End with "Pinned in the Skills Library."

### DMs and replies
- Match the energy of the message. Short to short, longer if they wrote a paragraph.
- Don't pitch in the first reply. Help first.
- Never copy-paste the same DM. Personalize the first sentence.

## The Voice Smell Test

Before publishing, read the line out loud. Ask:

1. Would I say this to a peer at a marketing meetup?
2. Is there a number, a tool name, or a verb in the first 6 words?
3. Did I avoid every banned word?
4. Could a SaaS marketing intern have written this? If yes, rewrite.
5. Is there an em dash? If yes, kill it.

If all five pass: ship.
